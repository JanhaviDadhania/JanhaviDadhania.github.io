organisation based on A Thousand Plateaus: Capitalism and Schizophrenia (1980)
by French philosopher Gilles Deleuze and psychoanalyst Félix Guattari
I want to organise every field we humans work on in a single system.
robotics, games, painting, voiceAI... from abstract to granular topics we work on.
because once i have it, i can track state of the art in each to understand where
humanity is right now and what are the next tasks we(VCs and rich people)
are definitely going to put money into.
I initially thought to start from "humanity", branch it into science and social-sciences,
sciences include all the subjects under it, social-science containing sub-branches like
faith, politics etc. But that is linear. world is complex ;). how could that system be right?
i found thinkers who have worked on exact thing. Their organisation system is a web
not tree. And i am adopting it.
Deleuze and Guattari argue reality is actually a Rhizome. A rhizome is a botanical term
for root systems like ginger, grass, or fungi. Any point can connect to any other point.
There is no "center" or "top level." If you cut a rhizome, it just sprouts somewhere else.
Instead of defining things by what they are intrinsically, the authors define things by
what they connect to and what they do. They call these connections Assemblages.
Crucially, they use the word "machine" in a very broad sense—not just metal gears or
code, but any system that produces an effect. A human mouth, food, and the social ritual
of dinner form an "eating machine." everything is a process. not absolute truth.
process is universal, objects are instances of process. A human with a smartphone is
fundamentally a different "machinic assemblage" than a human without one,
changing how both the human and the technology operate.
